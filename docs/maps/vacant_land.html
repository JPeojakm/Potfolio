Re-use vacant land

As many rust-belt cities grapple with the challenges of shrinking populations, the City of Cleveland is at a pivotal juncture. Vacant lands, a byproduct of this demographic shift, present both a challenge and an opportunity. This study aims to evaluate the effectiveness of Cleveland’s neighborhood revitalization strategy in repurposing these lands. Utilizing the Opportunity Index compiled by the Kirwan Institute for Race and Ethnicity, along with Geographically Weighted Regression analysis, we will assess the correlation between areas of high vacancy and low opportunity. This analysis is not only pivotal for local urban development but also forms a crucial part of our application for additional program funding from the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development. The hypothesis driving this research posits that Cleveland’s vacant land reuse program is adept at identifying areas with high vacancy rates and low opportunity indices, thereby targeting interventions where they are most needed.
